The Grelinette
Broadfork

The tool that aerates your soil without destroying it. Invented by Andre Grelin in the 1950s, built from forged steel to last decades.

The grelinette is a hand-powered soil aerator with 3 to 9 forged steel tines (14mm diameter) and two wide-set ergonomic handles. It loosens and lifts soil without inverting it — preserving the natural microbial ecosystem that keeps your garden thriving. A foundational tool of permaculture and no-dig gardening.

The 3-Phase Rhythm
1

Insert

Arms extended straight, tool tilted forward. Push the tines into the soil. The forward position naturally angles the tines backward, setting up the lift.

Keep arms straight — let body weight do the work.
2

Lift

Pull backward — not upward — to lever the soil up. The wide handles keep the motion above hip height. Your back stays completely straight.

Soil is lifted and loosened, never flipped.
3

Reset

Gently lower the soil back down without shaking. Slide the grelinette backward along the ground to reposition. Never lift the tool to move it.

Slide, don't lift — save energy for the next pass.
Why Use a Grelinette

Preserves Soil Biology

Aerates without inverting horizons. Topsoil bacteria stay at the surface where they thrive.

Zero Back Strain

Ergonomic handles above hip height. No bending, no kneeling. Your back stays straight throughout.

Faster Than Digging

Accomplishes aeration in a single pass. Significantly faster than traditional spade work.

Deep Weed Extraction

Optional arch attachment lifts soil higher, uprooting deep-rooted weeds like couch grass and bindweed.

Permaculture Aligned

Core no-dig principle: work with nature, not against it. No fuel, no noise, no emissions.

Built to Last Decades

Forged steel construction with 14mm tines. A one-time investment that outlasts any powered tool.

Choosing Your Tines
3–4
tines

Heavy clay soils or narrow raised beds. Easier to push in, less resistance.

5–6
tines

Most garden soils. The versatile choice for standard-width beds (60–80 cm).

7–9
tines

Light, sandy soils and wide beds. Maximum coverage per pass.

The No-Dig Principle

Traditional digging buries topsoil bacteria deep underground while bringing anaerobic subsoil to the surface. The grelinette does the opposite: it aerates without inverting, keeping the soil's natural layers intact. This single difference is why it's the preferred tool in permaculture, no-dig, and regenerative gardening worldwide.
